    • Marielle Pappaterra

    2002

    While I decided to major in Sociology at The College of New Jersey (TCNJ), I also minored in Fine Arts because I always had an affinity for the arts. As a child, I took classes in drawing, pottery, painting, and sculpture. I love how it became an outlet for me to expand my creative thinking and explore new ways to express different emotions. I was able to enhance my techniques and try new materials while studying at TCNJ.

    In my drawing classes, I used various forms of lead, charcoal, and ink. I improved my perspective drawing and finetuned how I developed concepts. In my painting classes, I was introduced to oil paints - I experimented with different application methods and how the medium interacts with other materials (like acrylic, denim, and glass).
