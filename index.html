• Paintings Print Making Animations

    • Marielle Pappaterra

    2002

    While I decided to major in Sociology at The College of New Jersey (TCNJ), I also minored in Fine Arts because I always had an affinity for the arts. As a child, I took classes in drawing, pottery, painting, and sculpture. I love how it became an outlet for me to expand my creative thinking and explore new ways to express different emotions. I was able to enhance my techniques and try new materials while studying at TCNJ.

    In my drawing classes, I used various forms of lead, charcoal, and ink. I improved my perspective in drawing and fine-tuned how I developed concepts. In my painting classes, I was introduced to oil paints. I experimented with different application methods and how each medium interacts with other materials (like acrylic, denim, and glass). In my printmaking class, I was exposed to various printing methods (like carving SpeedyCarve for stamps, carving linoleum for relif printing, screen printing, and riso printing). In addition, I have become very efficient with Adobe applications like PremierPro, Adobe Illisturate, and InDesign.
